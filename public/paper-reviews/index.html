Diffusion-weighted imaging theory Modelling white matter with spherical deconvolution: How and why? Diffusion and MRI https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2041910/pdf/13311_2011_Article_40300316.pdf Here’s an overview of how MRI works: Magnetic Alignment: The MRI scanner generates a strong magnetic field that aligns the protons in the body’s hydrogen atoms, which are abundant in water and fat. Radiofrequency Pulses: The scanner then emits RF pulses at a specific frequency that matches the resonance frequency of the aligned protons. These pulses provide energy, causing the protons to tip out of their alignment with the magnetic field (for example, a 90° RF pulse tips the aligned protons by 90 degrees from their inital alignment with the main magnetic field (B0), from $z$-axis to transverse plane ($x-y$ plane), this tipping creates a detectable signal in the MRI receiver coil)....
